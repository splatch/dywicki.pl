You might have a flat or appartment where you are living in, or a house inherited from your parents or bought. Maybe you was lucky enough to start a "greenfield development" and have built your own house literally from the ground. Finally, you might be looking for altrnatives to easier operate stuff you do on daily basis. Home is where we spent most of our time. The only place which can compete with amount of time occupied in life - is work. These two categories of places have completely different purposes. You might not be even aware of all systems which are there. They just do their job silently in the way that doesn't bother you. You realize they are there once they don't work as expected and start to annoy you. Have you ever thought why there is such amount of automation for commercial facilities where you spend less time than at home?

For quite long time most kind of automation was reserved for people who could afford it. With time passing by, cheaper hardware available on the market, it gets easier to experiment, adapt and take evolutionary approach. Possibly, if you already have some kind of automation, you might miss connectivity to new standard and "cloud connected" things you got for your home. Beauty of software defined approach is that you can verify idea without any serious investments.

While software is getting more powerful in its connectivities it gets also more difficult to maintain. All abstractions which are there to plug your vacuum, thermostat, light bulb or even a car are introduced to let you connect new items without need to redesign system from the scratch. Yet some complex ideas might suffer from that because they might get harder to map to software. This is some fair balance played by software engineers - with introducing higher levels of abstractions, they have to lower common denominator. A very generic software might offer same functionality as domain specific system, but it will require bigger effort.
